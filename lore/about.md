## The Premise

*Gravedancer to General: Anatomy of a Catastrophe* is a Star Wars fan
fiction series tracing the origin of **General Grievous** — the
cybernetic Supreme Commander of the Droid Army — back to the organic being
he used to be: **Qymaen jai Sheelal**, a Kaleesh warlord known, before his
transformation, as *the Gravedancer*.

This is a **pre-Clone Wars** story. There is no Separatist Alliance yet, no
droid armies, no Confederacy. There is only Kalee — a harsh, mist-wrapped
world locked in a grinding, generations-long conflict with the Huk, and a
single commander whose brilliance on the battlefield is matched only by a
grief that will not stop growing.

The series asks one question, over and over, in the space between legend
and atrocity:

> *How does a hero become a monster — and does anyone try to stop it?*

## The Setting

### Kalee

The Kaleesh homeworld is mountainous, rain-soaked, and ancestrally
warrior-driven. Kaleesh culture reveres the dead above the living; the
graves of ancestors are not memorials but *constituencies*, and a
warlord's authority rests on his ability to honor and avenge them. The
Kaleesh do not bury their dead so much as *consult* them.

Qymaen earned his epithet — **the Gravedancer** — for his uncanny ability
to move through battle as though choreographed by the dead themselves, and
for a habit of fighting near the grave-fields no other commander would
enter.

### The Huk War

The conflict with the insectoid Yam'rii (called *Huk* by the Kaleesh) is
the crucible. It is a war the Republic declined to end and the Jedi
declined to judge — a war that taught Qymaen that the galaxy's guardians
would watch a people starve and call it neutrality.

## The Arc

Each episode is a self-contained novella (~7,500 words) told across 3–8
*days* — discrete chapters following the campaign that defined that
episode's hunt. The series progresses from Qymaen as a mortal, masked
commander toward the slow, deliberate surgery that will remove everything
organic until only the General remains.

The transformation is not a single event. It is a series of choices, each
one defensible in isolation, each one making the next easier. The series
treats it as tragedy, not triumph.

## The Jedi

Each episode centers on a **target Jedi** — an original character (drawn
from the era, not the films) whose philosophy, species, and fate become
part of Qymaen's emerging doctrine. The [Jedi Index](../jedi-index.html)
catalogues them as they appear.
