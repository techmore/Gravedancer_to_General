---
title: "Glossary"
---

Terms from Kaleesh culture, the Huk War, and the wider galaxy as they
appear in the series. Add new entries as episodes introduce them.

### Kaleesh
The reptilian native species of Kalee — a warrior culture that venerates
its ancestors above the living and measures a leader by his devotion to
the dead.

### Kalee
The rain-wrapped, mountainous homeworld of the Kaleesh, locked in a
generational war with the Huk. Site of most of the series' action.

### Qymaen jai Sheelal
The birth name of the being who will become General Grievous. A Kaleesh
commander known as *the Gravedancer*.

### The Gravedancer
Qymaen's wartime epithet — earned for fighting among the grave-fields and
moving through battle as though guided by the dead.

### Huk
The Kaleesh name for the Yam'rii — the insectoid species with whom the
Kaleesh fought the Huk War. To call them *Huk* is already to take a side.

### Huk War
The grinding, generations-long conflict between the Kaleesh and the Huk
that the Republic declined to adjudicate and the Jedi declined to judge.

### The Gravedance
A Kaleesh martial discipline practiced near ancestral cairns, said to let
a warrior move in rhythm with the dead. Qymaen's signature.

### Jiwari lowlands
The flood-prone low ground of Kalee where Episode 1 is set — black mud,
basalt shelves, and old cairn fields.

### Lightsaber
The ceremonial and combat weapon of the Jedi Order — a blade of
contained plasma. Rare on Kalee; its presence signals Jedi involvement.

### Mirialan
A green-skinned, geometrically-tattooed near-human species native to
Mirial. Jedi from Mirial often follow contemplative traditions.

### The Living Force
A Jedi philosophical tradition emphasizing presence, instinct, and
attunement to the moment — contrasted with the Unifying Force's focus
on destiny and the grand pattern.

### General Grievous
The cybernetic Supreme Commander of the Droid Army of the Confederacy
of Independent Systems — the being Qymaen jai Sheelal will become. Not
yet extant during this series.

### CIS / Confederacy of Independent Systems
The Separatist alliance that will later wage the Clone War against the
Republic. Not yet formed during the events of this series.

### Republic
The Galactic Republic — the stalled, over-extended democracy whose
non-intervention in the Huk War shaped Qymaen's view of the galaxy.

### Jedi Order
The peacekeeping order of Force-sensitives who serve (theoretically) as
the Republic's conscience. Their neutrality in the Huk War is the wound
at the center of the series.
